Key Insight

India's unemployment rate was relatively stable around 8–12% throughout 2019. Starting March 2020 with the national lockdown, rates surged dramatically — peaking in April–May 2020 with some states crossing 40–75%. Recovery began from June–July 2020 onwards.

🌆 Area Comparison

Rural vs Urban Unemployment

How the lockdown impacted rural and urban areas differently across India.

🌾

Rural India

Avg: ~10.2%
  • Lower base unemployment pre-COVID
  • Agricultural sector provided some buffer
  • Reverse migration increased rural labour
  • Faster recovery post-lockdown
🏙️

Urban India

Avg: ~13.5%
  • Higher base rate — more formal jobs
  • Severely hit by industry shutdowns
  • Reverse migration worsened supply
  • Slower recovery — service sector lag
